A pulmonary nodule is a small round or oval-shaped growth in the lung, and it can be detected using a chest CT images. There are 5 main categories of nodules: solid nodule, ground-glass nodules, semi-solid nodules, calcified nodules, spiculated nodules. Countless pulmonary nodules are discovered each year during chest X-rays or CT scans. Most nodules are noncancerous (benign). A solitary pulmonary nodule is found on up to 0.2% of all chest X-rays films. Lung nodules can be found on up to half of all lung CT scans. Risk factors for malignant pulmonary nodules include a history of smoking and older age. This challenge aims to develop a machine-learning multi-class classification system, and my algorithm achieved an accuracy of 0.59 out of a max result of 0.66"
